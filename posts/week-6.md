## Part 1: Project Background and Brief Understanding

I understand that the purpose of this briefing is to require us to develop a genuine network application for a specific community, rather than merely creating a website with information. We need to identify a clear problem, determine the actual needs of the users, identify the core functional requirements, and explain why each design decision can help users complete tasks more clearly, safely and understandably when using the website program.

## Part 2: Comparison and Selection of Early Concepts
![](assets/images/1.1.png)

At first, we did not directly choose the "Medical Assistance Task Platform". Our group first discussed five possible directions, namely a health meal sharing platform, a hyperactivity support community, a communication platform for catering business users, a task platform for international students during their first week of residence, and a medical assistance platform. 

Subsequently, we created a "concept comparison matrix" to conduct a standardized evaluation of the five ideas based on their alignment with the brief, their task-oriented structure, and community value. We eliminated the topics that were too broad in scope. Ultimately, we chose the medical assistance platform, as this theme has a clear user base, real problems, and a manageable direction for the minimum viable product.

## Part 3: User Research and Problem Analysis
![](assets/images/1.2.png)

We conducted a user study using the methods of online ethnography and a short questionnaire. These methods enabled us to observe the problems in the users' actual online expressions. From this, we discovered that students were confused about where to go when they were ill, how the occupational health and safety plan worked, what medical expenses meant, and how to apply for a refund.

![](assets/images/1.3.png)
Based on this research, we have prepared a user pain point analysis table to understand what these problems actually mean to the users. From this table, we discovered that the pain points of users are not only related to the lack of information, but also lie in the deeper issue of the scattered medical information, the lack of local knowledge making it difficult to judge, and the difficulty in converting it into specific actions. Based on these findings, our website will only collect medical information. It needs to provide users with clear initial step guidance, display reliable places, and utilize peer experience to reduce uncertainty. 

We have set the target users as Chinese international students who have just arrived in Sydney. This helps us make the subsequent website more focused on their actual situations, language needs, and first medical experience.

## Part 4: Transforming Problems into Design Opportunities and Early Risks and Unknown Factors
![](assets/images/1.4.png)

We will transform the identified user pain points into design opportunities. Firstly, the website needs to consolidate the scattered information into a clear task path and provide a step-by-step guide for users to solve the confusion they may encounter during their first visit to the hospital. Then, for experiences that do not apply to everyone, we will design cards for sharing experiences on the platform and encourage users to openly share their experiences. 

We also identified potential risks, including medical accuracy, privacy issues, and the scope of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP). Taking these risks into account, we will make the platform more cautious.


## Personal Reflection

This project actually originated from a real problem I encountered during my study in Australia. When I was ill, sometimes I would think about taking painkillers first and enduring it for a while, as if seeing a doctor wasn't necessary and I could get through it. But later, I realized that this was not a truly safe or effective solution. Also, precisely because of this, I myself am one of the target users of this website. This project was not conceived out of thin air; rather, it was derived from the real problems I experienced in my daily life.

### Core design decisions:
Through the comparison matrix of concepts, we abandoned the directions that were too broad in scope or whose community value was not clear enough, and chose a more focused and more feasible medical assistance task platform that met the requirements of the MVP.
